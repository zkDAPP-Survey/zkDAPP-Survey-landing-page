01 — About the project

Replacing centralized survey agencies with cryptographic truth

Public opinion surveys are a cornerstone of democratic societies, yet centralized agencies suffer from trust and integrity issues. Operators may manipulate samples, suppress results, or fail to prevent Sybil attacks.

zkDAPP-Survey uses zkSNARK zero-knowledge proofs to allow any voter to prove eligibility — citizenship, age, region — without revealing their identity. All results are publicly verifiable on-chain.

  • 01Eliminate trust in centralized entities by using a public blockchain for transparent vote tallying
  • 02Prevent Sybil attacks through unique user verification based on government-issued verifiable credentials
  • 03Incorporate user attributes — age, geolocation, education — for filtered eligibility and result interpretation
  • 04Maintain full voter privacy using zkSNARKs generated client-side on the voter's smartphone
  • 05Keep survey participation accessible while preserving private, client-side proof generation on the voter's smartphone
02 — Mobile application

How To Use The App

Follow the core user flows step by step: first add credentials to Valera, then register for a vote, cast a private ballot, and finally create a survey with the guided builder.

How To Add Credentials To Valera ▾
This flow shows how credentials are requested and added to Valera during testing. In our demo setup, the app sends the request to our own `FIIssuer`, which verifies the submitted information and decides whether the credential should be issued.
Step 01 Home Page
Start from the Valera home page and tap `Load Data` to begin adding a new credential.
Step 02 Choose FIIssuer
Select `FIIssuer` as the source that will evaluate and issue the credential in the demo environment.
Step 03 Credential Information
Select the credential type, upload the passport image, and enter the personal details required for verification.
Step 04 Request Confirmation
Review the request and confirm that it should be sent to the issuer for evaluation.
Step 05 Approved Credential
If `FIIssuer` approves the request, the issued credential is returned and becomes available in Valera.
How To Register For Voting ▾
This flow shows how a participant registers for a survey before voting. The app first detects that the user is not yet registered, then guides them through authentication and selective disclosure of the credential data required for enrollment.
Step 01 Open The Survey
Start from the survey view where the app indicates that the current account still needs to register before voting.
Step 02 Begin Registration
Open the registration flow and review the initial information shown before the app requests proof data.
Step 03 Authenticate
Confirm your identity in the wallet flow so the app can proceed with the credential checks needed for registration.
Step 04 Choose A Credential
Select which credential should be used to prove that you satisfy the survey's eligibility requirements.
Step 05 Disclose Required Data
Approve disclosure of the required fields so the app can complete registration without exposing anything beyond the requested attributes.
How To Vote ▾
A guided flow for participants, from finding a survey to submitting a private vote on-chain.
Step 01 Explore Surveys
Browse active surveys and pick the one you want to answer.
Step 02 Survey Detail
Inspect the poll before voting so the context and requirements are clear.
Step 03 Verify Identity
Launch the identity check to prove you belong to the eligible voter set.
Step 04 Checking Eligibility
The mobile app generates and validates the proof locally before unlocking the ballot.
Step 05 Voting Screen
Select the answer and submit it without exposing your identity.
Step 06 Vote Submitted
The final state confirms the anonymous vote has been processed.
How To Create A Survey ▾
A guided flow for organizers, from opening the survey area to reviewing the final state after the survey is created and waiting to start.
Step 01 My Surveys
Open the organizer area and start from the surveys list where existing and draft surveys are managed.
Step 02 Create Survey
Enter the core survey information to begin the setup process for a new voting flow.
Step 03 Survey Questions
Write the prompts and structure the voting options the audience will receive.
Step 04 Voter Requirements
Define who is allowed to participate by selecting the credential-backed requirements for the survey.
Step 05 Review Survey
Inspect the complete summary and make sure the survey configuration is correct before submitting it.
Step 06 Survey Created
Submit the setup and confirm that the survey has been created successfully in the organizer flow.
Step 07 Pending Start
Return to the survey list and verify that the new survey appears in its waiting state before voting begins.

03 — System architecture

How it all fits together

Five components work in sequence — from credential issuance to on-chain vote tallying — with zero personal data exposed at any step.

01
Credential Issuer
  • Issues VC/PID credentials
  • Reviews submitted documents
  • Verifies applicant information
  • Issues credentials to the wallet
→
02
Identity Wallet
  • Stores verifiable credentials
  • Selective disclosure (SD-JWT)
  • Identity key pair generation
  • Signs vote messages
→
03
zkDAPP-Survey App
  • Survey creator + voter UI
  • Client-side ZK proof generation
  • Circom circuits
  • React Native
→
04
Smart Contracts
  • Creates surveys for the organization
  • Verifies zk-proofs
  • Adds voters to the census
  • Records anonymous votes on-chain
→
05
Vocdoni
  • Vochain consensus
  • Nullifier enforcement
  • Vote tallying on-chain
  • Anonymous voting protocol
→

SPRINT 01
Oct – Nov 2024
Research & Technology Exploration
Deep-dive into zkSNARK primitives, Circom circuit design fundamentals, and the EVM ecosystem. Evaluated zkSNARK libraries (snarkjs, Circom vs. ZoKrates). Surveyed related work in decentralized voting and digital identity. Defined the system threat model and privacy requirements.

SPRINT 02
Nov – Dec 2024
System Design & Architecture
Designed the high-level platform architecture: credential issuer, Identity wallet integration, smart contract layer, and backend relay. Defined the ZK circuit specification for eligibility proofs. Documented the nullifier-based double-vote prevention model and on-chain verification flow.

Smart Contracts & Credential Issuer
Implemented SurvFactory and SurvInstance Solidity contracts with VerifierRegistry and IdentityIssuerRegistry. Built the custom credential issuer with VCK IssuerAgent and OpenID4VCI. Forked the Identity wallet with extended signing capabilities — Poseidon hashing, identity key pair generation.
